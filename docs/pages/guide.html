Tea Disease Prevention & Treatment Guide

Comprehensive guide to prevent and manage common tea plant diseases.

Common Tea Leaf Diseases

Red Spot Disease

Causes: Fungal infection due to excess humidity.

Symptoms: Small red spots appear, leading to leaf discoloration.

Prevention
  • Prune infected leaves regularly.
  • Apply copper-based fungicides every two weeks.
  • Ensure proper air circulation in the plantation.
  • Avoid water stagnation around the roots.

Algal Spot

Causes: High moisture and poor drainage.

Symptoms: Reddish-brown spots with slimy texture.

Prevention
  • Improve drainage and avoid over-irrigation.
  • Use copper-based sprays for control.
  • Remove affected leaves promptly.
  • Maintain plant spacing to avoid overcrowding.

Gray Blight

Causes: Fungal spores spread by wind and rain.

Symptoms: Gray lesions leading to leaf curling.

Prevention
  • Apply broad-spectrum fungicides monthly.
  • Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization.
  • Regularly inspect plantations for early detection.
  • Maintain proper field sanitation.

Brown Blight

Causes: Excessive shading and wet conditions.

Symptoms: Irregular brown lesions with yellow halos.

Prevention
  • Ensure proper sunlight exposure.
  • Use potassium-rich fertilizers.
  • Prune the plantation to reduce humidity.
  • Apply neem oil as an organic preventive measure.

Helopeltis (Tea Mosquito Bug)

Causes: Insect infestation causing puncture marks.

Symptoms: Feeding marks leading to leaf drop.

Prevention
  • Introduce natural predators like ladybugs.
  • Spray neem oil or insecticides regularly.
  • Inspect plants weekly for early detection.
  • Maintain weed-free surroundings.
